Conditions I like to work in:

  • Some pressure helps me not get lost in pointless explorations
  • I don't mind interruptions. If it's not a good time I will tell people.
  • I usually get more done with a screen & keyboard, but can get more creative when not at my deks.

The times/hours I like to work in:

  • Usually be at work before 0900, if possible squeeze in a workout before or after.
  • Outside of that, I don't really know. I'd like to try and see if I can focus more later during the day (e.g. evening) or outside of the office.

The best ways to communicate with me:

  • Set deadlines, put up imaginary timelines. Doesn't have to be set in stone, but it helps me understand the what you expect. "We need to improve signup next quarter" sets a completely different scene than "We need to improve signup next release".

The ways I like to receive feedback:

  • Keep it constructive. "This sucks, it's just garbage. This is my feedback. Bye!" really doesn't help (it happened.) Tell me why, tell me what you think doesn't work, make it painful but make it clear.
  • If you think something should be obvious, tell it to me and make it clear: "I think you should know this without asking me."

Things I need:

  • Feedback. Tell me what you think, even if it's not pretty. See above.
  • Creative/Learning time. I need it to play and see what's possible/how far I can go.
  • Feedback.

Things I struggle with:

  • Huge introvert, I usually have a hard time around people I'm not familiar with. Eespcially asking questions. Working on it.
  • Sugarcoating. I want to make great things, not ok things.
  • When feedback gets personal.
  • Work/life balance. I don't figure out that I'm too tired/stressed until very late. I'm still figuring this one out.

Things I love:

  • Spontaneous design discussions. I love it when people get involved in design (not only about our product!)
  • Recognition/feedback from users. Good and bad.
  • When people offer alternative solutions.
  • Technical stuff. Code. Knowing how things work. Hacking bad prototypes together.
